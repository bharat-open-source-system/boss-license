## Scope — What This License Covers

BOSS License v1.0 is intentionally broader than most software licenses. It covers:

- **Software** — source code, binaries, scripts, applications, APIs, SDKs
- **Hardware Designs** — PCB schematics, circuit diagrams, Gerber files, KiCad/Eagle files
- **Firmware** — microcontroller code, FPGA configurations, embedded software
- **AI/ML Models** — model weights, training scripts, inference code
- **Documentation** — technical docs, tutorials, datasets, specifications
- **Any combination** of the above

This makes BOSS the first Indian open source license designed equally for **software AND hardware innovation** — enabling India's electronics, IoT, and robotics ecosystem alongside its software ecosystem.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is BOSS License recognized internationally?**
A: BOSS v1.0 is designed to meet the Open Source Definition (OSD) criteria and will seek OSI recognition. Until then, it is fully valid as a legal license instrument. It is compatible with all major permissive licenses.

**Q: I'm not in India. Can I use BOSS-licensed software?**
A: Yes, without any restrictions. The Indian jurisdiction clause only applies when Indian parties are in dispute. Your rights under the license are identical to MIT — completely free for any use.

**Q: I already published a library under MIT. Can I dual-license under BOSS?**
A: Yes. You can add BOSS as a second license option to any MIT-licensed work. This makes your library part of India's sovereign open source ecosystem from Day 1.

**Q: Does the BOSS Declaration apply to me as a private developer?**
A: No. The Declaration requirement is exclusively for Indian Government departments and public sector use. Private individuals, companies, and foreign users are not required to file any Declaration.

**Q: Can I use BOSS for hardware projects like Arduino libraries or KiCad designs?**
A: Yes. BOSS explicitly covers hardware designs, circuit schematics, and firmware — making it the ideal license for India's electronics and hardware innovation community.

**Q: What happens if I don't include the copyright notice?**
A: Including the copyright and license notice is the only requirement. If you fail to do so, you may lose the license protections. Always include the notice in distributed copies.
